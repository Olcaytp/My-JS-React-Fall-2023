- Can I use reserved keywords as variable names in JavaScript? No, you cannot use reserved keywords as variable names in JavaScript. Reserved keywords are specific words that have predefined meanings in the language, and they are used for various purposes like defining control structures, data types, and other functionalities. Attempting to use reserved keywords as variable names will result in a syntax error. Here are some examples of reserved keywords in JavaScript: - **`if`** - **`else`** - **`while`** - **`for`** - **`function`** - **`class`** - **`const`** - **`let`** - **`return`** - **`true`** - **`false`** - **`null`**
